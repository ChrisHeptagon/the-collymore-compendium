Quadratic equations can be solved using many methods including by using the Quadratic Formula.

The quadratic formula in question is $x=\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$

Quadratic equations are often represented in the form of the Quadratic standard form equation is $ax^2+bx+c=0$. Where $a\neq0$.


To use the Quadratic Formula, substitute the values of a, b, and c from the standard form equation into the expression on the right side of the quadratic formula, next simplify the expression and the result is the pair of solutions to the quadratic equation.

## Model Example 1

Solve $2x^2 + 9x - 5 = 0$, using the quadratic formula.

Step 1. Identify the a, b, and c values found within standard form.

Result: $a=2, b=9, c=-5$

Step 2. Substitute the standard form values into the quadratic formula.

Result: $x=\frac{-9\pm\sqrt{9^2-4*2*(-5)}}{2*2}$

Step 3. Simplify the previous equation.

Result: $x=\frac{-9\pm\sqrt{81-(-40)}}{4}$ to $x=\frac{-9\pm\sqrt{121}}{4}$ then to $x=\frac{-9\pm 11}{4}$

Step 4. Setup two equations that represent the positive and negative form of the prior equation.

Positive: $x=\frac {-9+11}{4}$ to $x=\frac {2}{4}$ then to $x=\frac {1}{2}$.

Negative: $x=\frac {-9-11}{4}$ to $x=\frac {-20}{4}$ then to $x= -5$.

Final Answer: $x=\frac {1}{2}$ and $x= -5$

## Model Example 2

Solve $3p^2 + 2p + 9 = 0$, using the quadratic formula.

Step 1. Identify the a, b, and c values found within standard form.

Result: $a=3, b=2, c=9$

Step 2. Substitute the standard form values into the quadratic formula.

Result: $p=\frac{-2\pm\sqrt{2^2-4*3*9}}{2*3}$

Step 3. Simplify the previous equation.

Result: $p=\frac{-2\pm\sqrt{4-108}}{6}$ to $p=\frac{-2\pm\sqrt{-104}}{6}$

Step 4. Factor $i$ from the negative radical.

Result: $p=\frac{-2\pm\sqrt{104}*i}{6}$

Step 5. Further simplify the radical.

Result: $p=\frac{-2\pm2\sqrt{26}*i}{6}$

Step 6. Remove the common factor of $2$ from the entire right side of the equation.

Result: $p=\frac{2(-1\pm\sqrt{26}*i)}{6}$ to $p=\frac{-1\pm\sqrt{26}*i}{3}$

Step 7. Rewrite the previous equation in standard complex number form of $a+bi$.

Result:  $p=-\frac{1}{3} \pm \frac{\sqrt{26}*i}{3}$

Step 8. Setup two equations that represent the positive and negative form of the prior equation.

Positive: $p=-\frac{1}{3} + \frac{\sqrt{26}*i}{3}$

Negative: $p=-\frac{1}{3} - \frac{\sqrt{26}*i}{3}$

Final Answer: $p=-\frac{1}{3} + \frac{\sqrt{26}*i}{3}$ and $p=-\frac{1}{3} - \frac{\sqrt{26}*i}{3}$

## Model Example 3

Solve $x(x+6)+4=0$, using the quadratic formula.

Step 1. Distribute the $x$ to get the equation in standard form.

Result: $x^2 + 6x + 4 = 0$

Step 2. Identify the a, b, and c values found within standard form.

Result: $a=1, b=6, c=4$

Step 3. Substitute the standard form values into the quadratic formula.

Result: $x=\frac{-6\pm\sqrt{6^2-4*1*4}}{2*1}$

Step 3. Simplify the previous equation.

Result: $x=\frac{-6\pm\sqrt{36-16}}{2}$ to $x=\frac{-6\pm\sqrt{20}}{2}$

Step 4. Further simplify the radical.

Result: $x=\frac{-6\pm2\sqrt{5}}{2}$

Step 5. Remove the common factor of $2$ from the entire right side of the equation.

Result: $x=\frac{2(-3\pm2\sqrt{5})}{2}$ to $x=-3\pm2\sqrt{5}$

Step 6. Setup two equations that represent the positive and negative form of the prior equation.

Positive: $x=-3 + 2 \sqrt{5}$

Negative: $x=-3 -2 \sqrt{5}$

Final Answer: $x=-3 + 2 \sqrt{5}$ and $x=-3 -2 \sqrt{5}$
